ABOUT US

Bitcoin is adecentralized digital currency that operates without a central authority or bank. It was introduced in 2009 by an unknown person or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto.

Transactions are verified by network nodes through cryptography and recorded in a public distributed ledger called a blockchain. Bitcoin allows peer-to-peer transactions globally, and its supply is limited to 21 million coins.

Many people use Bitcoin for investment, trading, or as an alternative to traditional currencies. As the first and most well-known cryptocurrency, Bitcoin has influenced the development of thousands of other digital currencies.
